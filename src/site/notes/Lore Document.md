# The World of the Artificial Suns

---

## I. The Premise

Long ago, an all-consuming darkness swallowed existence. It did not come from the void or from some ancient cosmic force — it came from a person. Everything that survived had to _earn_ its survival, adapting, evolving, or banding together under whatever light it could find. Everything that didn't either went extinct or was taken — twisted into a slave of the shadow.

Out of that collapse, a handful of survivor groups each found a different way to create an **artificial sun** — a source of light strong enough to hold the darkness back. The land around each sun became a sanctuary. Over generations, those sanctuaries grew into the **four major villages**, each built around the philosophy that won them their light.

The world's current age is the _aftermath_. The war against the darkness is already over, and the darkness already won. The villages aren't fighting to stop an invasion — they're living in the ashes of one, under borrowed light, while the thing that caused it all is still out there.

---

## II. The Lich

### Who He Was

He was born the **youngest heir** of a kingdom — last in the line of succession, with no real path to the throne no matter what he proved he could do. In a world where status was inherited rather than earned, his worth was decided before he was born, and it wasn't enough.

That early, permanent rejection curdled into obsession. He turned away from the throne entirely and toward something no one could take from him by birthright: power that didn't ask permission. He pursued immortality first — not out of philosophy or grand design, but because it was the one thing a bloodline couldn't gatekeep.

### The Fall

Immortality, once achieved, wasn't enough either. A world he didn't control could still take everything from him _indirectly_ — through war, time, decay, other people's ambitions. The obsession metastasized into something far larger: if nothing in existence should be allowed to threaten him again, then existence itself needed to become **his**.

He devised a ritual to claim the entire world as his domain. It triggered what was, at the time, simply known as **the War** — every kingdom of the old world rising against him at once. He won. Worse, the war itself seems to have _fed_ him — every death on the field became fuel, meaning resistance accelerated the collapse rather than slowing it. The old kingdoms didn't just lose. They were consumed, and what was left of them became the first shadow monsters: extensions of the Lich himself, harvesting souls to sustain him.

### What He Is Now

He maintains his youth — and possibly his sanity, his power, even his identity — by **stealing the bodies of those he finds to have the most potential.** What "potential" means exactly is still undefined (worth deciding privately so it can be foreshadowed consistently — magical aptitude? force of will? something about the soul itself?), but it's the metric by which he judges who is worth consuming.

He runs cult networks across the world whose purpose is to identify and deliver vessel candidates to him. This means:

- He is, in a very literal sense, still running the same exam that rejected him as a child — just with worse stakes, and he's the only one who gets to pass.
- His current face is never his original one. Players (and NPCs) may never know what he actually looked like before the War, only that whoever they're facing now is a borrowed mask over something much older.
- He could, in theory, already be hiding inside someone the party trusts.

**Open questions to resolve before play:**

- What specifically makes someone "high potential" in his eyes?
- What happens to a vessel once he's done with it — destroyed, discarded, locked away somewhere?
- Has the centuries of body-jumping degraded his mind over time, or has total victory left him cold and unchanging?

## III. The Old World

Before the darkness, the world's kingdoms were organized largely along **racial lines** — separate nations, separate cultures, sharp borders between peoples. When the darkness hit, those barriers evaporated almost overnight. Survival didn't care about bloodline, and neither did the people clinging to whoever could keep them alive.

This is the philosophical seed of everything that followed: the old world organized itself around what you were **born as**. The new world, born from the ashes of the War, organizes itself around what you can **prove** — through magic, ingenuity, faith, or strength. The four villages are, in part, a collective rejection of the old hierarchy.

The Lich, notably, never actually left that old logic behind. He just inverted it — he doesn't care about bloodline anymore, but he still ranks people and harvests the ones who qualify.

## IV. [[The Four Major Villages\|The Four Major Villages]]

### [[Village of Magic\|Village of Magic]]

- **Origin of light:** Discovered/created their sun through the study of magic.
- **Society:** Built entirely around magical study and education. Magic is the measure of a person's worth.
- **Government:** Enlightened Despotism.
- **The Exams:** At 18, every citizen sits three rigorous exams that determine their permanent social rank.
    - Pass → **Bright**
    - Fail → **Dim**
- _Thematic note:_ This is the clearest mirror of the Lich's own logic — a society that quantifies and permanently ranks human worth, just dressed up as meritocracy instead of conquest.

### [[Village of Technology\|Village of Technology]]

- **Origin of light:** Created their sun through technology.
- **Society:** Focused on creation, experimentation, ingenuity, and engineering. Education matters here too, but creativity and freedom of expression are core values in a way Magic doesn't allow.
- **Government:** Republic-Democracy.
- _Open thread:_ Is their freedom genuinely equal, or does access to resources/tools quietly gatekeep who gets to actually invent and rise?

### [[Village of Divinity\|Village of Divinity]]

- **Origin of light:** Their sun was a gift from the gods — this village was made _by_ divinity, not by mortal effort.
- **Society:** Naturalistic, deliberately avoiding anything that would damage their surrounding environment.
- _Open thread:_ What do they do when actually threatened? Do the gods intervene directly, or did they grant the sun and leave mortals to handle the rest?

### [[Village of Force\|Village of Force]]

- **Origin of light:** Survive using smaller, portable star sources rather than one great sun.
- **Society:** Nomadic and martial — every member trained to fight from the moment they can walk. They live by thievery and plunder, maintain one main settlement but rotate through many smaller ones to stay close to other villages.
- **Relations:** They rarely engage the other major villages directly.
- _Open thread:_ Why the restraint, given they could clearly cause trouble? Old treaty, mutual deterrence, or simple practicality (their star sources can't sustain a prolonged conflict)?

## V. [[The Order of Profane\|The Order of Profane]]

A group of leaders who walked away from their home villages after recognizing that the suns were being used by their governments as instruments of **control**, not protection. They are one of the only organized forces actively trying to fix the broken world rather than simply defending their own slice of it.

They operate outside the village power structures entirely — which makes them both trusted by desperate people who've fallen through the cracks of those systems, and viewed with suspicion by the village governments they walked away from.

_Established so far:_ They are proactive against the Lich's cult networks — willing and able to raid a cult compound and extract captives before they're delivered as vessels.

_Open threads:_ Internal structure, leadership, how members from four very different cultures (Magic, Tech, Divinity, Force) coordinate or disagree, what their actual long-term plan against the Lich looks like.

---

## VI. The Campaign Opening

The player characters wake up inside a cult compound, having each been kidnapped and marked as a **suitable vessel** for the Lich. Before they can be delivered, the **Order of the Profane** raids the compound and rescues them.

**Built-in consequences of this opening:**

- The party is already known to the Lich's network by name — a standing long-term thread (cult remnants, rival vessel-hunters, or the Lich's own curiosity about loose ends can resurface at any time).
- The Order is introduced as a credible, trusted ally from minute one, which earns them faster buy-in than an organization the party meets cold later.
- "Marked as having the most potential" is left deliberately open — exactly what that potential is for can be revealed gradually rather than explained up front.
